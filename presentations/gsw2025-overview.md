## GSW overview

* Events held across Greenland Nov. 7-14.
* QGreenland-Net team in Nuuk Nov. 9-16.

![](https://trey-stafford.github.io/images/greenland_science_week_conf.jpg){fig-align="center"}

## Why Greenland in 2025?

* Greenland Science Week is every ~2 years. This is year 2 of our 3-year
  project.
* Getting the word out about our project (e.g,. via [informative
  postcards](https://qgreenland.org/sites/default/files/2025-12/QGreenland%20DataONE%20postcards.pdf))
* Opportunity to engage with stakeholders and others involved with Greenland-focused science directly.
* Listening/Learning!

## QGreenland-Net Team In Greenland

* Trey Stafford and Robyn Marowitz of NSIDC attended as software developers
* Twila Moon of NSIDC attended as PI.
* Angie Garcia of the National Center for Ecological Analysis and Synthesis
  (NCEAS) attended as a community engagement/outreach coordinator.
* Ian Nesbitt of NCEAS attended as a data repository expert.


## Greenland Science Week

* Cryosphere side-meeting Monday 10th.
* Marine Research Seminar Tues. 11th.
* [GSW Conference](https://www.scienceweek.gl/program) Wed. 12th - Thurs. 13th.
* Stakeholder meeting at [Asiaq](https://www.asiaq.gl/en/) Fri. 14th.
* [Qaagitsi](https://arctichub.gl/activities/qaagitsi-greenland-science-week-2025/) Public Science Festival Fri. 14th.
* "Team building" excursion Sat. 15th.


## Ilulissat Science Forum Nov. 3-9

* Twila served as a moderator for a discussion about local impacts of climate change.
